About

LIOS labs is an arts and ecology research platform and residency programme in Pustynia Bledowska, Poland.

An interdisciplinary playground, dedicated to the sharing of regenerative social and environmental practices. In times of ecological and systemic crisis, LIOS labs’ intended community aims to activate an experimental field for new ways of living, learning and acting together. It’s mission is to foster creativity and catalyse agency through collective development of tools and tactics for inner, social and technological transformation.

From the desert gatherings of LIOS labs community emerges a decentralized network of artists, activists, researchers and other agents of change, who work together towards cocreation of sustainable futures. LIOS labs offer spaces for interdisciplinary collaboration and experiences that bring people closer to the feeling of unity within themselves and with the animate world.

The desert ecosystem

The main creative playground of LIOS labs is the Bledowska Desert, an anthropogenic formation in southern Poland that offers a warning against overexploitation of the natural environment. Participants of the residences confront a world where energy and water are assets of limited availability, and the power of the elements – wind, fire, sun and rain – is explicitly felt. In the harsh conditions of the post-industrial desert, likely threats of the future are confronted, inspiring the inhabitants of the temporary oases to share sustainable practices and work together.

VALUES

LIOS labs stands for love, sustainability and imagination. It explores new organizational structures, in which the participants of the gatherings, in the spirit of holacracy, cocreate the spaces and the rules by which the community functions.
