about OA27

OASIS 2027 (OA27) is a platform designed to facilitate interdisciplinary collaboration and knowledge exchange.

We focus on the problem of lands becoming deserts and water a scarce resource.

Why 2027?
According to scientific reports, the tangible effects of desertification will affect us as soon as in 2027...

OA27 aims to awaken environmental awareness and seek alternative strategies for coping with climate collapse, imagining a viable, sustainable future.

Focusing on processes of desertification, we aim to offer a translocal perspective on the issues of environmental and societal degradation through the prism of science, art and ecology.

about desertification

It is a process, by which fertile land becomes a desert - a dryland unable to sustain plants growth.

Desertification happens as a result of natural phenomena (i.e. drought), or human activities such as deforestation, or overexploitation of the soil.
41% of Earth’s land area are desserts
30% of the world's population lives in the drylands
13 EU countries are now prone to dessertification
8% of EU area is going to be affected

programme

OASIS 2027 programme explores alternative approaches for organising the future, through seeking deep adaptation strategies for coping with the climate collapse. The focus of the project lies on the processes of desertification, offering a translocal perspective on the issues of environmental and societal degradation through the prism of sciences and arts.

The Island

Residency part I: Mallorca, Spain: 07- 20th May 2022

Here the residents will research the local needs and problems, collaborating with people who are experiencing first hand changes to the land and the climate and developing solutions.

Location: We need a bit more on this. The project begins in Sóller, Mallorca, in the Tramuntana Mountain range. In this landscape, human agricultural and pastoral activity has deprived soil of biomass, causing it’s degradation, with current 1% of biomass in the soil. linked to the frequent intentional burning by farmers to advantage grazing land for the herds has lead to a degradation of the ecosystem, Most of the land in the Mediterranean sea basin which is is already in process of desertification.

The Desert
Residency II : Błędów Desert Klucze, Poland: 03-17 July 2022.

Followed by a two week acceleration phase in The Bledowska Desert, Poland. The two week period in the desert will be an opportunity to experience a live musing on what it would be like to live in the future.

Location: A unique sandy ecosystem, Pustynia Bledowska is an anthropogenic formation, created as a result of human activity. Desertification began as early as the XIII century, when the vast area of pine forests were cut down in order to expand the mine shafts of silver and lead in the region. Mass clearance of trees resulted in rapid revealing of underground glacier sand deposits.

The residency in the desert will simultaneously align with the LIOS labs programme, where cross collaboration is encouraged.

The City
OA27 Cultural Festival: Magdeburg, Germany: 17 - 31 October 2022

The third segment will unfold in the co-curation and development of an interdisciplinary festival taking place in Magdeburg, Germany.

The residents are expected to curate the festival over two weeks, with two venues to utilise their network, present the works, findings, projects, performances and workshops developed during the two research and development stages to the public.
